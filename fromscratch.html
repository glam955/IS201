What Is Actuarial Science?

Actuaries use math and statistics to measure risk and help companies prepare for the future — especially in insurance.

The Actuarial Exam Pathway

  1. Exam P — Probability
    • Probability theory and distributions
    • Univariate and multivariate random variables
    • Pass rate: ~40%
  2. Exam FM — Financial Mathematics
    • Time value of money, annuities, bonds
    • Interest rate derivatives
    • Pass rate: ~42%
  3. Exam FAM — Fundamentals of Actuarial Mathematics
    • Replaced IFM, LTAM, and STAM (effective 2023)
    • Covers short-term and long-term actuarial math
    • Pass rate: ~40%
  4. Exam ALTAM — Advanced Long-Term Actuarial Mathematics
    • Advanced life contingencies and survival models
    • Benefit reserves and pension mathematics
    • SOA fellowship pathway
  5. Exam ASTAM — Advanced Short-Term Actuarial Mathematics
    • Advanced loss distributions and severity models
    • Credibility theory and ratemaking
    • CAS/P&C pathway
  6. Exam SRM — Statistics for Risk Modeling
    • Regression, time series, principal components
    • Introduction to machine learning methods
  7. Exam PA — Predictive Analytics
    • R programming in a 5-hour open-book exam
    • GLMs, decision trees, cluster analysis

Branches of Insurance

  • Life & Health
  • Property & Casualty (P&C)
  • Reinsurance
  • Pensions & Retirement Benefits
